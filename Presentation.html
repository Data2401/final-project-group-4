Introduction

Have you ever wondered why some cars cost significantly more than others? Using the Car Features and MSRP dataset from 1990-2017, we set out to answer our central question:

What factors most strongly influence a car’s price?

Questions

Domain Question:

What factors most strongly influence a car’s price (MSRP)?

Sub‑Questions: 1. What is the average price for two‑door vs. four‑door cars?

  1. How do engine size, horsepower, or MPG relate to MSRP?

  2. How does drivetrain type (FWD, RWD, AWD) influence average MSRP?

  3. Are there clusters or subgroups of cars (luxury vs. economy)?

Why this Question ?

  • Cars are a significant purchase for most people to make
  • Price is misunderstood
  • Help a buyer understand what drives costs to make a better decision

Key Variables

  • Engine.HP – Horsepower

  • city.mpg / highway.MPG – Fuel efficiency

  • Driven_Wheels – Drivetrain type

  • Number.of.Doors – Vehicle design

  • Market.Category – Luxury, performance, exotic, etc.

  • MSRP – Manufacturer’s Suggested Retail Price

Data Cleaning — Step 1: Standardizing Drivetrain Labels

data <- data %>%
  mutate(Driven_Wheels = gsub("all wheel drive", "AWD", Driven_Wheels),
         Driven_Wheels = gsub("rear wheel drive", "RWD", Driven_Wheels),
         Driven_Wheels = gsub("front wheel drive", "FWD", Driven_Wheels),
         Driven_Wheels = gsub("four wheel drive", "4WD", Driven_Wheels))

Standardized the Drivetrain label by their abbreviation - Well known - Looked better on the eye for graph

Data Cleaning — Step 2: Fuel Type Consolidation

data <- data %>%
  mutate(Engine.Fuel.Type = case_when(
    Engine.Fuel.Type == "premium unleaded (required)"    ~ "premium unleaded",
    Engine.Fuel.Type == "premium unleaded (recommended)" ~ "premium unleaded",
    Engine.Fuel.Type %in% c("flex-fuel (unleaded/E85)",
                             "flex-fuel (premium unleaded recommended/E85)",
                             "flex-fuel (premium unleaded required/E85)") ~ "Flex-Fuel",
    TRUE ~ Engine.Fuel.Type
  ))
  • Multiple verbose variants of the same fuel type were being treated as separate categories

Data Cleaning — Step 3: Filtering Rare & Unknown Types

data <- data %>%
  filter(!Transmission.Type %in% c("UNKNOWN", "DIRECT_DRIVE")) %>%
  filter(!Engine.Fuel.Type %in% c("", "natural gas", "electric"))
  • Transmission types labeled UNKNOWN provide no analytical value
  • Natural gas and electric vehicles follow different pricing structures than standard vehicles

Data Cleaning — Step 4: Dropping Rows with Missing Values

data <- data %>%
  filter(!is.na(Number.of.Doors),
         !is.na(Engine.Cylinders),
         !is.na(Engine.HP))
  • Rows missing horsepower, cylinders, or doors cannot contribute to price analysis

Data Cleaning — Step 5: Removing Outliers

data <- data %>%
  filter(MSRP <= 1000000) %>%
  filter(highway.MPG <= 200)
  • MSRP > $1M — exotic vehicles that compress the price scale for all other cars
  • Highway MPG > 200 — a data entry error that distorts MPG visualizations
